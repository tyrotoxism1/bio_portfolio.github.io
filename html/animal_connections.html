Quiz Question:

Patterns are used to organize and classify organisms. Explain why classification of organisms is important, and describe an example where incorrect identification of an organism could be a problem.

My answer:

The classification of organisms is so important because there are just so many organism, and with that there are many sub groups within the general group. There are hundreds upon thousands of classifications and sub-classifications made for each individual living being on this Earth. And this is important because it allows for there to be identification of the unique and individual species or organism. Classification, when incorrect, can be dangerous, an example of the dangers is when looking at the plant called 'Wild Carrot' it looks harmless, but there is another plant called 'poison hemlock' which is not so harmless. This incorrect identification can lead to someone being poisoned because of this. Another could be that it could ruin a data set if one species is grouped together with another. This would lead bad data points, and if the scientists never realized the mistake could mislead people who read and take for a fact the publishing of whatever published study.
