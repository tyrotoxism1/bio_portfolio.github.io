Mastery #2:

In the second media piece, I've used a quiz answer to show mastery of science concepts. The quiz question asks about 2 different ways a species can mutate. The quiz answer shows mastery of the outcome because it provides an example to break down how the 2 different concepts of mutation can occur within a species.

Quiz Question:

Describe two scenarios: one in which a new species forms by allopatric speciation, and one in which a species forms through sympatric speciation.

My answer:

Let's say there were a species of trees. In the first scenario, the trees are split apart due to an earthquake that splits the land in two, with some kind of ravine in the middle. Over time, this species would mutate, but they would each mutate differently depending on these new locations. Maybe one of areas of trees receives more light while the other receives less now. This would eventually most likely cause differing mutation between the two. In the second scenario, maybe humans planted a different kind of species of tree in the same area as this hypothetical tree species. Over time there may be some of the original trees that are able to breed with the new species of trees creating a new kind of tree that may not be able to breed with the original species of tree.
