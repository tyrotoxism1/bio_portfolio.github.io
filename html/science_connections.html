Mastery #2:

For the second media piece, I've included a news summary I did for my GEO GEO 308 class. This news summary, summarizes a study done on global warming and it's effects on animals. This summary shows mastery for the science connections outcome since it serves to inform myself and others about global issues beyond just science. This media piece also shows mastery in the relation of science and global change/effects.

News Summary

Publication Date: November 23, 2020
Name of Media: Science Daily
Title: Global warming likely to increase disease risk for animals worldwide
Summary:
A study conducted by University of Notre Dame, University of South florida and University of Wisconsin-Madison found supporting evidence for a phenomenon known as “thermal mismatch hypothesis”. The article explains how this hypothesis describes how the greatest risk for infectious disease is when the climate of a cold-adapted animal becomes warm, or when a warm-adapted animal’s environment becomes colder. The article references a quote from a professor of Notre Dame, pointing out how important this research is considering the current pandemic the world is seeing. The article continues to talk about how the data was collected and how the researchers correlated the data to a conclusion. The researchers found that the data they collected strengthened the hypothesis previously mentioned, and that when coupled with models of global climate change projections, they were able to predict where the risk of animal infectious disease might change the most. The article sums up by stating how global warming will likely shift infectious disease away from the equator, but will see an increase in the highland tropics, where the temperate and cooler regions of the planet are.
