Animal Concepts Page

The animal concepts outcome describes the behavior, characteristics and ideas that help humans understand animals better. Mastery of this outcome can be shown by describing an animal's phyla, behaviors, and habits. Media showing other details about an animal's environment or behaviors also shows mastery of the outcome.

Mastery #1:

For the first piece showing mastery of animal concepts, I have a media piece describing one of the main behaviors of my dog. This demonstrates mastery of animal concepts because it shows an understanding of what the bark is for, and whether it is learned or innate.

Behavior: Barking

My dog, barks and stares at me with a look in her eye. The barking is quite loud and high pitched, but that’s her normal bark. This bark is a form of communication behavior, while I cannot completely understand what she exactly wants every time, I can infer since she has to go out at certain times, and based on how long ago she was last taken out. She also will stand near the door to go out for the bathroom. If she wants food she will bark near her food bowl, and walks will be close to the front door of the house. These are all forms of communication behavior since it is exchanging the desire to do something with her barks. This behavior is very likely to be learned since she has associated that we will not take her out without her letting us know somehow, and we respond the quickest when she barks. It’s not innate for her to bark to go to the bathroom since when she was young, she didn’t bark to go out, she just went, which was lovely. So we had to train her to go to a certain place outside, and to bark to let us know. So since it was training that our dog went through it is a learned behavior. This behavior doesn’t really impact her fitness, she will survive if she barks and we don’t take her out, her barking is only done because she was trained to bark, if she didn’t we could maybe tell if she near one of the doors, or she could just go in the house and still survive. The barking doesn’t significantly affect her fitness, it really is only for the cleanliness of the house.

Quiz Question:

Compare artificial selection to natural selection and provide multiple examples of artificial selection in animal species.

My answer:

Artificial selection is when humans select desirable traits of parents, whether it be with plants or animals, that then are passed on the children. This is done quite commonly in human farming industry, as it allows for humans to create more "ideal" plants and animals with more controlled outcomes. Some examples would be Corn, Brassica, and animals such as pigs, cows, and sheep. With Natural selections, the selection happens, well, naturally. Traits are passed down based on survival, in certain environments, certain traits are better suited for that scenario so they are passed on, or selected. When looking at examples of artificial selection in animals, most think of how cows, or most farm animals have been selected by humans to have better traits. In the case of cows, they are breed to have better meat quality, and so that it's not too fatty, but also breed so that each of the cows will produce a lot of meet per body, they are fed much more than they would naturally eat on their own without human tempering. Another example would be in dogs, many dogs have been cross bred, and for a very long time, dogs have been with humans where humans have also messed with the natural selection with dog breeding. One specific example of dog breeding, is the Saluki, this dog was bred to be very fast and lean.
